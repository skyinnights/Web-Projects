Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist who led initiatives worldwide that contributed to the extensive increases in agricultural production termed the Green Revolution.

Born in 1914 in Iowa, Borlaug focused on developing disease-resistant, high-yield crops that greatly impacted global food production, saving over a billion people from starvation. He was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 for his efforts in fighting hunger.
